# Anti-Patterns

These are the most common ways this skill can fail.

## 1. Persona Theater

Bad:

- Writing polished character stories from 1-2 posts
- Inventing tidy motivations the sources never stated

Better:

- Name search behavior, trust logic, and blockers

## 2. Evidence Laundering

Bad:

- Treating researcher notes as if they were user quotes
- Treating paraphrases as equally strong as direct quotes

Better:

- Keep direct evidence, paraphrase, and inference visibly separate

## 3. Platform Equals User

Bad:

- Assuming Reddit users are one persona and Xiaohongshu users are another

Better:

- Use platform as one signal, not the identity model

## 4. Generic Product Advice

Bad:

- "Build community"
- "Improve onboarding"
- "Add trust features"

Better:

- Tie product changes to a specific search-stage blocker or trust gap

## 5. Average-User Collapse

Bad:

- Merging distinct search patterns into one “typical user journey”

Better:

- Keep multiple pattern-specific flows

## 6. Hiding Unknowns

Bad:

- Pretending a thin sample proves market truth

Better:

- Output candidate patterns and explicit next evidence to collect

## 7. Data Dump Instead Of Decision Memo

Bad:

- Leading with pages of raw material

Better:

- Lead with conclusion, keep traceability in the appendix
